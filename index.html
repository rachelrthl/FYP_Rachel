1.1 The Protagonist: Scale Team Cloud Sales

Sarah is a Cloud Sales Representative managing 1,000 SMB accounts (50-500 employees) at a leading cloud infrastructure provider in ASEAN. She sells cloud computing services ($10K-$100K deals) through a high-volume, partner-led Scale team model designed to reach thousands of accounts cost-effectively.

Her challenge: With a $2M annual quota and 1,000 accounts to cover, Sarah faces an impossible question every Monday morning: Which accounts should I call this week? Guessing wrong means wasting hours researching dead-end accounts while high-intent buyers‚Äîposting jobs, announcing funding, evaluating vendors‚Äîslip through to competitors. She's not failing from lack of effort; she's failing because human bandwidth cannot scale to 1,000 accounts.

1.2 Current State: The Research Bottleneck

Sarah's Manual Process (Per Account):

  • LinkedIn: Company updates, job postings, executive activity (3 min)
  • Google: Funding news, growth signals (3 min)
  • Website: Technology initiatives, careers page (2 min)
  • Internal systems: Engagement history (2 min)

Total: 10 minutes per account

The Mathematical Problem:

  • 1,000 accounts √ó 10 min = 167 hours to research portfolio once
  • Available time: 40 hrs/week √ó 30% prospecting = 12 hours/week
  • Actual coverage: 72 accounts/week = 7% of portfolio

The Consequence: 93% of accounts receive zero proactive attention. Sarah prioritizes using crude filters (company size, industry) rather than actual buying intent.

1.3 The Problem: Signal Blindness Costs Millions

What Sarah Misses Each Week:

While researching 72 accounts, across her remaining 928 accounts:

  • ~15 companies post cloud engineer job openings
  • ~8 companies announce funding or expansion
  • ~12 executives post about digital transformation
  • ~10 companies attend cloud/tech events

Total: ~45 high-intent buying signals go undetected weekly.

"Coverage was limited to three to five accounts per week per rep, leaving large portions of their ICP untouched... I spend hours researching each account, manually checking LinkedIn, websites, funding, tech stack." ‚Äî Sales leaders (Salesmotion case studies, 2024)

  • Internal propensity scores: Black-box algorithms reps don't trust
